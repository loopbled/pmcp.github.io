Writing detailed and clear documentation

In this section, we will look at the key aspects of writing effective documentation for your plugin. Good documentation makes it easier for other developers and users to interact with your code, making the process of using your plugin clearer and more enjoyable.

1. Why you need documentation:

  • User Orientation: Helps users understand how to use your plugin and what features it provides.
  • Simplifying integration: Documentation makes it easier for other developers integrating your plugin into their projects.

2. Key elements of the documentation:

  • Installation and Customization: Provide clear instructions for installing and basic configuration of your plugin.
  • Functionality Description: List the main features of the plugin and step-by-step instructions on how to use them.
  • Code examples: Include code examples to illustrate key concepts and utilization of the plugin's API.
  • Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ): Answer frequently asked questions to prevent repetitive support requests.

3.Layout and Structure:

  • Clear structure: Divide documentation into logical parts for user convenience.
  • Use headings and labeling: Clearly highlight key information by using headings, lists, and other formatting elements.

4. Update documentation:

  • Make sure your documentation is up to date: Regularly update your documentation according to changes in your plugin.
  • Update Notifications: Alert users of important changes and new features.

5. Greeting Feedback:

  • Welcome Feedback: Invite users to provide feedback and report bugs.
  • Community Support: Create a forum or other communication channel where users can share experiences and help each other.

6. Examples and Case Studies:

  • Real Examples: Provide real examples of how your plugin is used in different scenarios.
  • Practical Assignments: Invite users to complete practical assignments to gain a deeper understanding of the plugin's capabilities.

7. Provide contacts:

  • Support and Feedback: Provide contact information for support and feedback.

Creating detailed and clear documentation helps make your plugin more accessible and appealing to a wide audience.

Selecting and explaining the selected license

In this section, we will look at selecting and explaining the license for your plugin. Licensing plays a key role in determining the rules for using, distributing, and modifying your code.

1. Why you need a license:

  • Legal protection: The license defines users' rights and responsibilities with respect to your plugin.
  • Distribution: Determines if and how your plugin can be distributed.

2. License Types:

  • MIT: Allows free use, modification, and distribution, as long as the original license is included.
  • GPL: Obliges derivative works to also be open source and free.
  • Apache: Provides broader source code rights, including the right to use in patents.

3. Key Points to Explain:

  • Purposes of the license: Describe what your goals are in choosing a particular license.
  • Rights and Restrictions: Explain what rights your license grants to users and what restrictions they must abide by.
  • Compatibility with other licenses: If your plugin uses code with a different license, specify compatibility and possible restrictions.

4. Example of license explanation:

  • * License: MIT **
  • Purpose: To provide freedom of use and distribution with minimal restrictions.
  • *Rights: Users are free to use, modify, and redistribute the plugin, provided they retain a copy of the license.
  • Limitations: The license is not responsible for any damage or loss resulting from the use of the plugin.

Choosing and explaining the license is important for users to clearly understand the relationship with your plugin.
